# Softadastra Community Events

This document defines how community events related to Softadastra are organized and conducted.

Events are considered an extension of the foundationâ€™s communication and governance. Their purpose is to deepen understanding, not to generate hype.

## Purpose of events

Softadastra events exist to:

- explain architectural decisions
- share implementation experience
- discuss constraints and trade-offs
- gather informed technical feedback

Events are not marketing campaigns or product launches.

## Types of events

### 1. Technical talks

Focused presentations on:

- architecture and design choices
- sync, networking, and resilience topics
- lessons learned from reference implementations

Talks prioritize depth and correctness over accessibility.

### 2. Design discussions

Small-group discussions intended to:

- review architectural proposals
- challenge assumptions
- surface edge cases

Design discussions are moderated and goal-oriented.

### 3. Workshops and hack sessions

Hands-on sessions focused on:

- building reference components
- experimenting with sync and networking primitives
- testing failure scenarios

Workshops are exploratory, not feature-driven.

## Event principles

All Softadastra events must follow these principles:

- **Technical depth first** : no superficial content
- **Honesty about limitations** : no overpromising
- **Respect for long-term vision** : no pressure to pivot
- **Small and focused** : scale is not the goal

## Audience

Events are primarily intended for:

- systems engineers
- backend and distributed-systems developers
- architects and researchers

They are not optimized for general audiences or non-technical stakeholders.

## Frequency and scale

Softadastra favors:

- infrequent but meaningful events
- small groups with high signal
- long-form discussions

Large conferences or mass events are not a priority.

## Transparency and recordings

When appropriate:

- talks may be recorded
- materials may be published publicly

However, some design discussions may remain private to preserve focus and depth.

## Relationship to the roadmap

Events do not drive the roadmap.

They inform understanding, surface issues, and refine implementation details, but do not override architectural direction.

## Summary

Softadastra community events exist to cultivate a thoughtful, technically rigorous ecosystem.

By keeping events focused, honest, and architecture-driven, Softadastra ensures that community engagement strengthens the foundation rather than diluting it.
