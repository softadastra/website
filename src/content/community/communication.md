# Softadastra Community Communication

This document defines how Softadastra communicates with its community.

Communication is treated as part of the architecture: clarity, precision, and consistency are required to protect the foundation over time.

---

## Communication principles

All communication around Softadastra follows these principles:

- **Clarity over hype** : no exaggerated claims or marketing language
- **Accuracy over speed** : it is acceptable to communicate slowly
- **Depth over reach** : we prioritize understanding over visibility
- **Consistency over novelty** : messages must align with the documented vision

---

## Target audiences

Softadastra communicates primarily with:

1. Systems and infrastructure engineers
2. Backend and distributed-systems developers
3. Researchers and architects
4. Long-term technical investors

Communication is not optimized for mass-market appeal.

---

## Official communication channels

Softadastra may use the following channels:

- GitHub (issues, discussions, pull requests)
- Technical documentation repositories
- Long-form technical articles
- Public talks and recorded presentations

Short-form social media is secondary and must not redefine the narrative.

---

## Tone and style

The tone of Softadastra communication must be:

- professional
- technical
- calm
- precise

Avoid:

- hype-driven language
- urgency framing
- promises of disruption

Softadastra speaks in terms of constraints, trade-offs, and architecture.

---

## Announcements

Announcements should:

- describe what changed
- explain why it matters architecturally
- reference relevant documentation

Announcements must not oversell progress or imply guarantees beyond what is implemented.

---

## Community discussions

Discussion spaces are meant for:

- technical questions
- architectural clarification
- thoughtful proposals

They are not venues for:

- marketing
- speculative hype
- off-topic promotion

Moderation prioritizes signal over volume.

---

## Transparency

Softadastra aims to be transparent about:

- architectural decisions
- trade-offs and limitations
- long-term intent

Uncertainty is acceptable. Ambiguity is not.

---

## Summary

Communication in Softadastra exists to preserve clarity and trust.

By prioritizing technical accuracy and long-term consistency, Softadastra ensures that its community understands not only what is being built, but why it is being built.
